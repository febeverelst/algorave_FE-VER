 ### Coaching feedback

 During our coaching session, we reviewed my progress in the project, what I had already accomplished, and what the prospects were. We discovered that I have _many ideas_ in my head, but that there is _no concrete, written plan yet._ At the moment, I am enthusiastically experimenting at random, and that is still working, but in the coming weeks, I will definitely need to start finding some structure. 

Together, we drew up a short to-do list:
- Refine goal ‚Üí too vague
	- Cookbook with recipes
- Planning 3.5 weeks ‚Üí not super detailed
- Continue to document all the little things
	- What has been tried, what works, what doesn't, what will work?
- Think about visuals (end-to-end ‚Üí low priority)

We looked at possibilities for the live performance together and came across a web-based P2P collaboration editor for live coding sounds and images called [FLOK](https://flok.cc/s/scared-ivory-cardinal-4eb6e111). We tried it out and you can use strude and hydra together on two different windows. Of course, this still doesn't solve the problem we have: now I have to code both visuals and sound when I really just want to focus on the music. But it's something to keep in mind.

After the consultation, my coach came up with the _AKAI MPD226, a USB MIDI pad controller_. I had already been thinking about using something like this because it gives more options in terms of sound control. And now that I have my hands on one im excited to start using it.


### Feedback Conclusions.

1. I need to have a very clear idea of what I want to include in the cookbook. Recipes for basslines, drum stacks, etc.

2. Make a not-so-detailed plan for the next three and a half weeks. Mainly a kind of overview so that I have an idea of what still needs to be done and happen.

3. The documentation is going in the right direction, but now it seems more like I'm just summarising at the end of the day. Every trial and error has to be included, otherwise it looks like I'm not doing anything, and that contradicts my toggle.

4. I don't need to worry about the visuals at the moment. I'll have enough work learning strudel and all the tricks of the trade.


### Current plan of Action

### AKAI MPD226

I didn't know how to get started with this device, so I just looked it up on Google. That's how I found this site: https://www.akaipro.com/usb-pad-controller-with-rgb.html

The video helped a bit but at the end I had to ask ChatGPT to help me and that gave me the following steps that made it very easy to install the pad controller:
1. Plug the MPD226 into your MacBook
2. Open GarageBand (default app on iOS)
	- Choose ‚ÄúEmpty Project‚Äù
	- Select ‚ÄúSoftware Instrument‚Äù
	- Click Create
3. Load a drum instrument
	- Open Library
	- Choose a "Drum Kit"
4. Test the pads, 

The pads lit up, which meant they recognised the touch, but I didn't hear any sound. Fortunately, Jules was sitting opposite me and he told me that the pads probably had some kind of velocity coding (which was correct), so I just had to hit them harder. I tried that for a while, but then my fingers started to hurt, so I looked up how to adjust the sensitivity. After that, it was much more comfortable to work with.
